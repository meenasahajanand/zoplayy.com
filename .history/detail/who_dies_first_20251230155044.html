In "Who Dies Last?", 3 to 6 players take on the roles of characters trapped in a perilous situation, such as being stranded on a sinking ship, trapped in a haunted mansion, or stranded on a deserted island. The objective is simple yet intricate: survive longer than your opponents. The catch is that only one character can make it out alive, and the rest will meet their fictional demise.
The game is played through a series of rounds, each consisting of three phases: Decision, Negotiation, and Resolution.
1. Decision Phase:
During this phase, players select two cards from their hand. One card represents their choice to "Stay" and the other to "Leave". The "Stay" card indicates a player's intention to survive until the end, while the "Leave" card signifies their desire to escape the perilous situation.
2. Negotiation Phase:
In this phase, players engage in intense negotiations, forming alliances, breaking promises, and trying to deduce the intentions of their opponents. Players can exchange cards, make deals, and use their powers of persuasion to manipulate others into making decisions that align with their own survival strategy.
3. Resolution Phase:
After negotiations, all players simultaneously reveal their selected cards. The tension builds as the choices are unveiled. Characters who chose to "Leave" immediately exit the situation and are out of the game. The remaining characters who chose to "Stay" have successfully survived the current round.
As the rounds progress, the game becomes increasingly intricate. Players must consider their relationships, alliances, and adversaries as they strive to outmaneuver their opponents. Trust can be a valuable asset, but betrayal might also be a viable strategy to ensure one's own survival.
To enhance the gameplay experience, "Who Dies Last?" incorporates character-specific abilities and event cards. These abilities can tip the scales in favor of particular players, introducing an element of strategy and unpredictability. Event cards introduce unexpected twists, forcing players to adapt their plans on the fly and adding an extra layer of suspense.
The game continues for several rounds, with survivors from each round advancing until only one character remains alive. The last character standing is declared the ultimate winner, having outwitted, outmaneuvered, and outlasted their opponents.
