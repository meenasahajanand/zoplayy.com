Novice players in FIFA Soccer often face a steep learning curve when it comes to defending and shooting effectively. However, with some practice and the right techniques, they can improve their skills and enjoy a more competitive gaming experience. In this guide, we'll break down the basics of defending and shooting in FIFA, focusing on the most important aspects for beginners.
For novice players, using the contain button (usually 'X' on Xbox or 'Square' on PlayStation) is a good starting point. It makes your player jockey the opponent and keep them at bay. Remember, you don't always have to tackle; simply containing can be effective.When it's time to tackle, be patient and time it correctly. Press the tackle button (usually 'Circle' on PlayStation or 'B' on Xbox) when you're sure you can win the ball. Sliding tackles are riskier, so use them sparingly.Maintain good defensive positioning. Don't rush your defenders out of position, as this can leave gaps in your defense. Instead, use the right stick to switch between players and anticipate your opponent's moves.
Use the Teammate Contain button (usually 'R1' on PlayStation or 'RB' on Xbox) to get your AI-controlled players to press the opponent. Be cautious not to overcommit, as skilled opponents can exploit this.Experiment with your team's defensive custom tactics. You can set your team to play aggressively or defensively, depending on your preference. Start with balanced settings and adjust as needed.
For novice players, it's best to start with basic shots. Use the shoot button ('Circle' on PlayStation or 'B' on Xbox) when you're close to the goal and have a good angle. Remember to aim the left stick toward the target.When you're one-on-one with the goalkeeper, finesse shots (R1/RB + Circle/B) can be more accurate and less likely to be blocked. Use them when you have a clear path to the goal.
For long-range efforts, use a power shot. Hold down the shoot button ('Circle' on PlayStation or 'B' on Xbox) longer to add more power to your shot. Be cautious with this, as it can result in inaccurate shots if not timed properly.Once you're comfortable with the basics, you can try timed finishing. Press the shoot button a second time just before your player makes contact with the ball for a more accurate and powerful shot. This requires precise timing but can be highly effective.FIFA offers skill games that can help improve your shooting skills. Spend some time in these modes to practice different shot types and scenarios.Pay attention to your players' traits and specialties. Some players are better at certain types of shots, so use them accordingly.Like any skill, practice makes perfect. Play matches against AI or friends to refine your shooting skills and learn from your mistakes.
In FIFA Soccer, defending and shooting effectively are essential skills for success. Novice players should focus on mastering the basics, such as using the contain button for defense and using basic shots for scoring. With practice, patience, and a willingness to learn, you can gradually improve your abilities and enjoy a more satisfying gaming experience.
