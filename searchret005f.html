    Minecraft is a wildly popular sandbox video game developed and published by Mojang Studios, a Swedish game development company. Markus Persson, also known as "Notch," initially started working on the game in May 2009. The game's first public release occurred on May 17, 2009, and it gradually gained popularity, eventually becoming one of the best-selling video games of all time. The concept for Minecraft was inspired by Persson's love for games like Dwarf Fortress, Dungeon Keeper, and Infiniminer. He wanted to create a game that combined elements of survival, creativity, and exploration in a procedurally generated, blocky world. Minecraft's early development saw several experimental versions, each introducing new gameplay elements and mechanics. In 2011, Mojang Studios was founded, with Markus Persson as its founder, to further develop and support Minecraft.
